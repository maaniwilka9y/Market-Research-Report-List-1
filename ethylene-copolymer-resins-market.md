<p><p>Ethylene copolymer resins are categorized into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I typically includes ethylene-vinyl acetate (EVA) copolymers, known for their flexibility and transparency, making them suitable for applications like adhesives, films, and packaging. Type II encompasses ethylene-acrylic acid (EAA) copolymers, which offer enhanced adhesion and compatibility with various materials, often used in automotive and construction industries. These classifications highlight the distinct properties and applications that cater to diverse industrial needs and consumer products.</p></p>
