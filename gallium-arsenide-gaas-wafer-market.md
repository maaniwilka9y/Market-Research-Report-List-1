<p><p>The Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) wafer market is primarily divided into two types based on the growth techniques: LEC (Liquid Encapsulated Czochralski) grown GaAs and VGF (Vertical Gradient Freeze) grown GaAs. LEC grown GaAs wafers are known for their high structural quality and are often used in optoelectronic applications. In contrast, VGF grown GaAs wafers offer better uniformity and lower defect densities, making them suitable for high-performance electronic applications. Both types cater to diverse technological needs across the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
