<p><p>The egg stabilizer market includes three main types: cream of tartar, sugar, and starch. Cream of tartar, a byproduct of winemaking, helps stabilize egg whites, enhancing volume and texture in meringues and soufflés. Sugar also plays a vital role, improving the stability and structure of whipped egg mixtures while adding sweetness. Starch, commonly used in various forms, aids in thickening and stabilizing, making it essential in puddings and baked goods. Together, they enhance culinary applications across the food industry.</p></p>
