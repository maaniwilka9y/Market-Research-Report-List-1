<p><p>Biological valves, commonly referred to as bioprosthetic valves, are heart valves made from animal tissue, typically porcine or bovine, used to replace damaged or diseased heart valves. Technological advancements in valve manufacturing and implantation techniques, such as minimally invasive procedures, are also contributing to market expansion. The rising acceptance of bioprosthetic valves over mechanical ones due to their biocompatibility and lower complication rates is another key factor.</p><p>Additionally, there is a noticeable trend towards the development of innovative valve designs and hybrid valves that combine the benefits of both mechanical and biological materials. An increase in research and clinical trials focused on improving valve performance and longevity further supports market growth. <p><ul><li>Surgical Valve</li><li>Interventional Valve</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biological valve market is primarily categorized into surgical and interventional valves. Surgical valves are typically implanted during open-heart surgery to replace damaged heart valves, which are often made from animal tissues or biologically compatible materials. Interventional valves, on the other hand, are designed for minimally invasive procedures, such as transcatheter valve replacement, allowing for valve implantation without traditional surgery. Both types aim to restore proper heart function and improve patient outcomes, addressing various heart valve conditions effectively.</p></p>
